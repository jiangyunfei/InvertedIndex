Relevant work started in 19th century. To make it easier to understand, it starts out as a philosophy question, rather than a computer science problem or a biology problem.
Alexander Bain ﬁrst mentioned the connectionism ideas in [1]. Even earlier, he offered an interpretation of mental phenomena within an association framework. Then, in his book, he argues a brand new point of view stating memory is a model capable of ”putting required stuff together” rather than ”storing everything beforehand”. He named this memory structure as ”neural groupings”, as a predecessor of ”neural net- work” in our world, and further explained that under his view, connected nerve ﬁbers are channeled to different parts of the network under different simulations.
